Malappuram

Places to explore

Thunchan Parambu

As we observe Vijayadashami Day this month, Thunchan Parambu at Tirur in Malappuram district will again be in the spotlight as people from across the state come here for Vidyarambham or Ezhuthiniruthu, the ceremony in which children are initiated into learning. Thunchan Parambu or Thunchan Memorial Trust and Research Centre is the birthplace of Thunchath Ezhuthachan, the father of Malayalam language. In his own distinctive poetic style, Ezhuthachan rendered the stories of great epics such as the Mahabharata and the Ramayana.

Pazhayangadi Mosque

Pazhayangadi Mosque, Kondotti, is located 18 km east of Manjeri, on the way to Malappuram in Kerala. The three-day-long Valia Nercha feast at this 500-year-old Muslim pilgrim centre, celebrated in February/March, is a local cultural event. This mosque with its lovely white dome is unique as it embodies facets of Mughal architecture.

Trikandiyur shiva temple at Tirur

Parasurama, the legendary creator of Kerala, is said to have installed the idol at the ancient temple located near the town of Tirur. Devotees from far and wide throng the Trikandiyur Shiva Temple at Tirur in Malappuram district. The vibrant history, charming architecture and powerful deity all range among the attractions of this temple.
